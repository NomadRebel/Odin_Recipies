Paneer Tika

Ingredients:

For the Marinade:

  • 250g paneer (cut into 1-inch cubes)
  • 1/2 cup thick yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon mustard oil (optional, for added flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cream (optional, for a richer marinade)

For Cooking:

  • 1 tablespoon oil (for grilling or pan-frying)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Onion rings and lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade:
    • In a large mixing bowl, add the thick yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, kasuri methi, and salt.
    • Add mustard oil (if using) and fresh cream (if using). Mix everything until it forms a smooth, thick marinade.
    • Gently fold the paneer cubes into the marinade, making sure they are well-coated with the spice mixture.
    • Cover and refrigerate the marinated paneer for at least 30 minutes (ideally 2 hours or overnight) to let the flavors infuse.
  2. Cook the Paneer Tikka:
    • You can cook the paneer tikka using a grill, oven, or pan-fry them.
    • Grilling Method:
      • Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
      • Thread the marinated paneer onto skewers, ensuring the cubes are spaced out evenly.
      • Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the paneer is golden brown and slightly charred.
    • Oven Method:
      • Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
      • Place the marinated paneer cubes on a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
      • Cook for 15-20 minutes, flipping the paneer halfway through, until it’s cooked through and slightly charred.
    • Pan-Frying Method:
      • Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
      • Add the marinated paneer cubes to the pan and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown and slightly crispy on the edges.
  3. Serve:
    • Once cooked, remove the paneer from the skewers (if using) and place them on a serving platter.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve with onion rings and lemon wedges.
    • Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind chutney on the side.
