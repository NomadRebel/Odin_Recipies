Dal Makhani

Description:

Dal Makhani is a famous North Indian dish made with whole black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma), cooked slowly in a creamy, buttery gravy. It's rich, flavorful, and goes perfectly with naan or rice. Here's a simple recipe to make Dal Makhani at home:

Ingredients:

For the Dal:

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 4 cups water (for cooking the dal)
  • Salt to taste

For the Gravy:

  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 tomatoes, pureed (or 1 cup canned tomato puree)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-2 green cardamoms
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 2 tablespoons butter (for garnish)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Dal:
    • Wash the black lentils and kidney beans thoroughly. Soak them in water for at least 6-8 hours or overnight.
    • After soaking, drain the water and add the dal and rajma to a pressure cooker.
    • Add about 4 cups of water and some salt. Cook on medium heat for 4-5 whistles, or until the dal and beans are soft and fully cooked. If using a pot, cook the dal and beans on low heat for 1-2 hours, adding more water as needed, until the dal is soft and creamy.
    • Once cooked, mash the dal slightly with a spoon or the back of a ladle for a creamy texture.
  2. Prepare the Gravy:
    • Heat ghee and oil together in a large pan or kadhai.
    • Add cumin seeds, cardamoms, and the cinnamon stick. Let them sizzle for a few seconds.
    • Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
    • Add the pureed tomatoes, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook the mixture on medium heat for 10-12 minutes until the oil starts separating from the masala.
  3. Combine Dal and Gravy:
    • Once the tomato masala is cooked, add the cooked dal and kidney beans to the pan.
    • Stir well and add about 1 cup of water (or more if you prefer a thinner consistency). Let the dal simmer on low heat for 15-20 minutes so the flavors can blend together.
  4. Make It Creamy:
    • Add the heavy cream to the dal and stir well. This gives the dal its rich and creamy texture.
    • Add kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) and stir. Let it simmer for another 5-7 minutes.
  5. Garnish and Serve:
    • Finish off with a dollop of butter on top for that extra richness.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
