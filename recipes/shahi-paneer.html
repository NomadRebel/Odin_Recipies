Shahi Paneer

Description

Shahi Paneer is a rich and royal North Indian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cream, and a variety of aromatic spices. It's known for its rich, flavorful, and creamy texture. Here's a simple recipe to make Shahi Paneer at home:

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer (cottage cheese)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/4 cup yogurt (whisked)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green cardamoms
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Saffron strands (optional, soaked in warm milk for flavor and color)
  • Cashew nuts or almonds (optional, ground into a paste)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Paneer: Cut the paneer into cubes. If you prefer soft paneer, you can soak the cubes in warm water for 10-15 minutes to soften them.
  2. Prepare the Gravy:
    • Heat ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds, cardamom, cinnamon stick, and cloves. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
    • Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
    • Stir in the pureed tomatoes, turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala (about 5-7 minutes).
  3. Make it Creamy:
    • Add the whisked yogurt and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly to avoid curdling.
    • Add the milk and stir well. Let it simmer for a few minutes to form a smooth, creamy gravy.
  4. Add Paneer and Final Spices:
    • Add the paneer cubes to the gravy and gently mix to coat them in the sauce.
    • Add the garam masala, fresh cream, and saffron milk (if using). Stir gently and cook for 5-7 minutes on low heat, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors.
  5. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.

Enjoy your delicious Shahi Paneer! It's a royal treat, rich in flavor and texture.
